THE PROBLEM

The AI ecosystem moves hourly.
Nobody has a single source of truth.

Scattered signals

Model rankings on HuggingFace. Status on 6 different pages. SDK downloads across PyPI, npm, crates, Docker. Research on arXiv. Community on Reddit, HN. No single view.

No citations

Existing dashboards show numbers without linking to the upstream source. You see "GPT-4o: #3" but can't verify it. Trust is assumed, not earned.

No distribution

Every competitor requires you to visit their site. Nobody pushes the signal to where you already are — your inbox, your feed, your API.

THE PRODUCT

Live dashboard. Daily briefing. Open API.

Every number on gawk.dev traces to a clickable public source. If we can't cite it, we don't ship it.

THE TRUST CONTRACT

What makes Gawk different

  • Every number links to its public source
  • No invented scores, no LLM-generated rankings
  • Zero LLM calls in the production data pipeline
  • Sanity ranges pre-committed before data ships
  • Source down = grey card + last known value + timestamp
  • Public transparency page lists every source by name

This is a compliance-grade data product

Most AI dashboards explain methodology at the dashboard level. Gawk cites at the cell level. Every number, every time, every source.

Typed source registry: 1,200 lines of source contracts in data-sources.ts — sanity checks, rate limits, caveats, verification dates
